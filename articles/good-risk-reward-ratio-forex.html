What Is a Good Risk Reward Ratio in Forex?

A good risk reward ratio in forex is often around 1:2 for many traders, meaning the planned reward is twice the risk. However, the best ratio depends on win rate, strategy quality, and execution.

Educational note: This guide is for learning and risk planning only. It is not financial advice.

What does 1:2 risk reward mean?

A 1:2 risk reward ratio means the potential profit is twice the potential loss. If a trader risks $20, the planned reward is $40.

What is a good risk reward ratio?

A common risk reward ratio is 1:2, but a good ratio depends on win rate, trade quality, and execution. A high ratio is not useful if the setup rarely reaches the target.

Risk reward and win rate

Risk reward ratio must be considered together with win rate. A lower risk reward may require a higher win rate, while a higher risk reward can tolerate a lower win rate but may be harder to achieve consistently.

How to use risk reward before entry

Before entering a trade, mark the entry, stop loss, and take profit. If the potential reward is too small compared with the risk, the trade may not be worth taking.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is 1:3 better than 1:2?

Not always. A 1:3 target gives a higher potential reward, but it may be less likely to hit depending on market conditions and strategy.

Can risk reward ratio make a losing strategy profitable?

No. Risk reward helps with planning, but the trading setup still needs a real edge and consistent execution.

Should every trade have the same risk reward ratio?

No. Different market structures may offer different reward potential, but each trade should have a clear plan before entry.
