What Is Risk Reward Ratio?

Risk reward ratio compares the amount a trader is willing to risk with the potential reward of a trade. For example, a 1:2 risk reward ratio means the trader risks one unit to potentially gain two units.

Why Risk Reward Ratio Matters

A clear risk reward ratio helps traders plan trades before entering the market. It also helps avoid emotional decisions because the stop loss and take profit levels are already defined.

Example

If a trader risks $20 on a trade and the potential profit is $40, the risk reward ratio is 1:2. If the potential profit is $60, the ratio is 1:3.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is 1:2 risk reward good?

A 1:2 risk reward ratio is commonly used because the potential reward is twice the risk. However, it still depends on win rate, trade quality, and execution.

Can I trade with 1:1 risk reward?

Yes, but a 1:1 ratio usually requires a higher win rate to remain profitable over time.

Does high risk reward guarantee profit?

No. A high risk reward ratio does not guarantee profit. The trade still needs a valid setup, good execution, and proper risk control.
