What Is Drawdown in Forex Trading?

Drawdown is the decline from a trading account's highest value to a lower value after losses. It is usually shown as a percentage and helps traders understand how much their account has dropped during a losing period.

Why Drawdown Control Is Important

A large drawdown makes it harder to recover. For example, a 10% drawdown requires about 11.1% gain to recover, while a 50% drawdown requires 100% gain to return to the original balance. This is why serious traders focus on protecting capital before chasing returns.

Prop Firm Drawdown

Prop firm traders usually need to follow strict daily loss and maximum drawdown rules. A drawdown calculator can help estimate how close an account is to a risk limit before a trader continues trading.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is a good maximum drawdown?

A lower drawdown is generally safer. Many conservative traders try to keep total drawdown below 10%, although the right limit depends on strategy and account rules.

Is 20% drawdown dangerous?

A 20% drawdown can be difficult to recover from and may indicate that risk per trade is too high or that the strategy is going through a poor period.

How do I reduce drawdown?

Traders can reduce drawdown by lowering lot size, reducing risk per trade, avoiding revenge trading, and stopping trading after reaching a daily loss limit.
